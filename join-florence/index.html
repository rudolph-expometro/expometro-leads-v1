In 1504, Florence unveiled Michelangelo's David for the world to see. More than five centuries later, your artwork could be the one capturing people's attention.

Between Santa Maria Novella Station and the Basilica lies a pedestrian tunnel used by more than 50,000 people every day.

Normally, its LED screens display advertising.

But for one extraordinary day, the tunnel will be covered in art — across the walls and ceiling. And if you decide to join us, your artwork will be part of it.

Welcome to the Immersive Art Tunnel.

On Saturday, November 28, ExpoMetro will transform this space into an immersive Art Tunnel, with artworks covering the walls and ceiling.

Visitors will literally walk through a world of art, surrounded by artworks from artists across the globe.

The exhibition will be accompanied by music, creating an unforgettable immersive experience.

It all started with one simple idea: replace advertising with art.

In December 2018, I invited artists to exhibit their work on poster displays throughout the Paris metro.

It was our very first exhibition. That's how ExpoMetro was born.

Since then, more than 6,000 artists from over 100 countries have joined the movement in some of the world's most iconic locations.

What inspires me most is the incredible energy that emerges when artists from around the world come together.

About Me

My name is Rudolph.

After a traditional career in leading auction houses and prestigious art galleries, I decided to challenge the rules of the art world. I was tired of seeing so many talented artists lost online or unable to exhibit beyond their own homes.

Today, my mission is simple: get your art out of your phone and into the public eye.

If you believe that art can change the world, join our movement and help make art accessible to everyone, right in the heart of the city.

I can't wait to discover your artwork and hopefully meet you at our next exhibition.
